**Surrey Fire years-in-business language:** Always use "nearly 30 years" or "almost 30 years". Never "25+ years" or "over 25 years". The anchor number is 30 (est. 1997, 2026 = 29 years). Updated in brand.md and business.md 2026-05-20.

**Why:** User correction — 25+ is now stale; nearly 30 anchors on the rounder, more impressive number and is more accurate.

**How to apply:** In any Surrey Fire copy, callout, or description referencing years of experience — use "nearly 30 years" (descriptions) or "Nearly 30 Years Experience" (headlines).

---

All RSA headlines and descriptions use title case — capitalise every word except short joiner/connector words (the, and, or, but, in, on, to, at, for, a, an, by, of). First word of every headline and description always capitalised regardless.

**Why:** User confirmed this as the house style for Contrelle ad copy 2026-05-27.

**How to apply:** Apply to all RSA drafts across all clients unless the client has a different confirmed style. Check before saving any email or CSV output.

---

RSA structure formula: 2 CTAs + 2 social proof in headlines; 2 CTAs + 2 social proof in descriptions. Apply to all RSA drafts as a default structure check before finalising.

**Why:** User confirmed this as the standard formula 2026-05-27.

**How to apply:** Before saving any RSA draft, count CTAs and social proof assets in both headlines and descriptions. Flag any gaps and suggest replacements from the weakest remaining slots.

---

Always fill RSAs to maximum assets (15 headlines, 4 descriptions) when the ad is going for client approval. Partial builds leave no headroom for disapprovals.

**Why:** Google disapproves individual assets not whole ads. Having 15 headlines means 2-3 can be flagged and the ad still serves with strong coverage. User explicitly asked for maximums when going into approval round.

**How to apply:** Default to 15 headlines + 4 descriptions for any RSA going to client or to Google for review. Only build fewer if user explicitly asks for a lighter draft.

---

Avoid trailing filler words in headlines that don't add standalone meaning. Example: "Pelvic Floor Retraining Too" — "too" implies something else was said before it, which doesn't work in a headline that may appear alone. Correct version: "Pelvic Floor Retraining".

**Why:** User corrected "Pelvic Floor Retraining Too" → "Pelvic Floor Retraining". The word "too" requires prior context that a standalone headline doesn't have.

**How to apply:** Before finalising RSA headlines, check any trailing connective words ("too", "also", "as well", "either") — if the headline doesn't read cleanly in isolation, remove or rephrase.

---

Contrelle health policy — avoid stats and clinical claims in headlines where possible. The 68% dryness stat is the only confirmed safe one, but it is already heavily used across the account — do not add it to new RSAs unless explicitly requested. Do not use percentage claims, "up to X%" framings, or paraphrased clinical stats. Default to benefit and lifestyle language over claims.

**Why:** Multiple disapprovals and near-disapprovals on Contrelle account. User wants clean approvals to build case for policy restriction removal.

**How to apply:** In Contrelle RSA drafts, replace stat headlines with social proof (no numbers), lifestyle, or benefit language. Flag any headline that makes a bodily sensation or clinical claim as HIGH risk.

---

When building RSAs for healthcare/medical device accounts (especially Contrelle), flag approval risk by level for each asset. Google aggressively flags: NHS references, hospital names, GP/physician mentions, clinical statistics as headline claims.

**Why:** Contrelle account was previously disapproved; Google misclassifies the vertical as healthcare. User wants to know which assets carry disapproval risk so they can manage expectations with the client.

**How to apply:** In the summary table, include a Risk column (LOW/MED/HIGH) and note which LOW-risk assets provide fallback coverage if the HIGH-risk ones are flagged.
